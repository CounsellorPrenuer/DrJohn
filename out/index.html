Emotional Preparation for the Interview – Speak to Yourself

The worst-case scenario is not getting hired.

I have nothing to lose and everything to gain.

This is practice, and I will learn from it.

This is one of many opportunities, and I will use it to grow.

I will be honest and frank.

I will be patient.

I will stay calm and not get stressed.

I will remain positive and hopeful until the end.

I will not speak negatively about my present or previous employer.

Researching the Company

Researching the company is one of the most important steps before an interview. It helps you answer confidently and shows genuine interest in the organization.

Read the company website carefully.

Understand their products, services, and values.

Learn about recent achievements and news.

Understand the job description clearly.

Interview Process – 4 Stages

Application Review: Employers review resumes and cover letters to shortlist candidates.

Initial Screening: A phone or video interview to assess basic qualifications.

In-Person Interview: Meeting with managers or a panel to discuss experience and skills.

Follow-Up: Feedback, further rounds, or a job offer.

HOW TO DO WELL IN A GROUP DISCUSSION

Try to grab the opportunity to speak first and introduce the topic.Example Opening (5 Sentences):“Good morning friends. The topic given to us is ______. This has been the center of discussion in many forums and in the media. This topic is very important in our lives, and I am glad we are discussing it today. In my view, I believe ______. Now let us hear the views of others.”

Listen carefully and look for the right opportunity to speak (do not interrupt too often).

Agree with others and add examples or experiences.

Disagree politely and support your view with logic.

Present both sides of the issue.

Intervene if the discussion becomes aggressive.

Cooperate and show leadership.

Do not corner or make fun of participants.

Encourage quiet members to speak.

Use examples and real experiences.

If you did not start, try to conclude the discussion.

Starting a GD – Introducing the Topic

“Friends, the topic given to us is ______. This is one of the most discussed topics.”

OR

“This is a controversial topic and has been widely debated in the media and public forums. As every issue has two sides, this topic will also have different viewpoints. Our aim should be to reach a balanced view. The way I understand this topic is ______. Some may say ______, while others may say ______. Let us proceed.”

Intervening When the Discussion Goes Out of Control

“Friends, I think we need to control this discussion. Otherwise, everyone will speak, and no one will listen. Let us speak one by one. Let us also hear from those who have not yet spoken. I am fine speaking at the end.”

Concluding the Discussion

“For the past 10–15 minutes, we have had a very interesting discussion. We have heard diverse views. The overall consensus appears to be ______. However, we must consider the points raised by others as well. Therefore, we can conclude that:

______”

Self-Learning UsingChatGPT – Your Knowledge Assistant

ChatGPT is one of the easiest tools to use. The key is knowing how to ask the right question (Prompt Engineering).

It can:

Write emails

Summarize long essays

Create presentations

Teach subjects

Generate sales leads

Write software code

And much more

It is a must-learn tool for students and employees.

AI is now integrated into search engines like Google, Bing, and Microsoft applications such as Excel, Word, and Outlook.

Easy Way to Start:Download Bing on your mobile phone and start asking questions by voice.

Optimizing Questions (Prompt Engineering)

Assign a role: “You are an expert web developer.”

Give a clear task: “Develop an e-commerce website to export handicrafts.”

Set format: “Use simple language and bullet points.”

Limit length: “Respond within 500 words.”

Types of Prompts

Chain-of-thought prompts

Role-play prompts

Conversational prompts

Prompt priming (provide context)

Avoid bias and use neutral language

Examples of Prompts

Create a banner for an online summer sale with a beach background and the text “Summer Sale – Up to 50% Off.”

As a CRM executive, help a customer who cannot log into a website.

Write a Python function that returns the sum of all even numbers in a list.
